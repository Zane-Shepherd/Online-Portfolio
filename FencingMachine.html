Fencing Machine

This fencing machine was a design project from my second year of University. The goal of the project was to design a machine that could theoretically be capable of performing a varying sequence of attacking and defensive maneuvers that was complex enough not to be predicted by the user.

The focus of the project was mechanical design, with no physical manufacturing being performed, and the limited electrical elements being left out of the design. The challenge of the project was to acheive effective randomness without using a CPU or random number generator. The proposed solution utilized a music-box like mechanism to action a series of cam and followers that would depress electrical switches in a set order. These switches would connect to the motors actioning the foil in order to perform the prescribed movements. Though not true randomness, the mechanism could contain enough differenet sequences to be fully unpredictable.
